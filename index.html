In Process Quality Control

About IPQC

In-Process Quality Control (IPQC) ensures that product quality is maintained during the manufacturing process. It involves inspecting and testing Semi-Finished Goods (SFG), performing system static testing and PDI as per the PPC schedule, and monitoring CTQ parameters for new builds or design changes. IPQC is responsible for maintaining inspection records, raising NCRs, performing ERP transactions and CoC & UIN generation, and ensuring calibration of measuring instruments. It also supports audits, drives continuous improvement and team upskilling, participates in DRS, kick-off meetings, ECR, and PFMEA reviews, and ensures adherence to 5S and SQDC standards in inspection areas.

Roles and Responsibilities of IPQC

  • Conduct SFG (Semi-Finished Goods) testing and inspection as per defined quality standards and procedures.
  • Ensure dry run trials and documentation updates for any new builds or design modifications, including identification and monitoring of CTQ (Critical to Quality) characteristics.
  • Execute Static Tests and Pre-Delivery Inspections (PDI) as per the Production Planning and Control (PPC) schedule and ensuring timely status update to all stakeholders/departments.
  • Identify skill gaps within the team and drive upskilling or competency development initiatives through training or resource support.
  • Propose and implement continuous improvement initiatives to enhance testing efficiency, accuracy, and product quality.
  • Ensure proper documentation, control, and traceability of all work order, inspection reports, test data, and system files.
  • Raise NCRs (Non-Conformance Reports) & on time closure of NC actions.
  • Actively participate in key organizational processes such, DRS (Delivery Requirement Sheet), Kick-off meetings, and other project reviews.
  • Actively participate in key organizational processes such as ECR (Engineering Change Request) and PFMEA review (Process failure mode effect analysis)
  • Perform ERP transactions for SFG movements, generate CoC (Certificate of Conformance), and ensure data accuracy within the system.
  • Support internal and external audits by providing evidence, records, and process demonstrations.
  • Ensure calibration and availability of measuring instruments used during inspection and testing activities.
  • Monitor and ensure adherence to 5S, SQDC standards in the inspection and testing areas.

Incoming Quality Control

About IQC

Incoming Quality Control (IQC) ensures that all materials and parts received from suppliers or internal sources meet the required quality standards before being used in production. It involves inspecting incoming parts, recording test results, raising nonconformance reports for rejected items, and coordinating with departments like SCM, Stores, PPC, and MRB for timely inspection and resolution. IQC also develops inspection procedures and CTQ parameters, maintains calibrated measuring instruments, updates inspection status in ERP, supports new build trials and design changes, participates in project reviews, and ensures audit findings are addressed.

Roles & Responsibilities of IQC

  • Incoming inspection of inwards parts which are received from Supplier, MRB, CS & Production.
  • Regular communications with SCM, Stores, PPC, MRB & CS for the timely completion of received parts inspection.
  • Ensure dry run trials are conducted for all new builds and design modifications.
  • Record the inspection results in test reports.
  • Raising nonconformance reports for the rejected parts & communicate the same to SQA, SCM & PPC.
  • Reject or quarantine non-conforming materials.
  • Authority to Pass/Fail/Hold the received material with valid reasons.
  • Developing inspection and test procedures along with associated CTQ (Critical to Quality) characteristics.
  • Perform ERP transactions related to material movements and inspection status updates.
  • Ensure calibration, validity, and availability of measuring instruments used for inspection and testing.
  • Drive upskilling and competency development through training, mentoring, or resource support.
  • Actively participate in key organizational and project-related processes, including: * DRS (Delivery Requirement Sheet) * Project kick-off meetings * Design and production reviews * ECR (Engineering Change Request)
  • Ensure audit findings related to IQC are addressed and closed within defined timelines

Flight Team Quality Control

About FTQC

Flight Team Quality Control(FTQC) ensures that the finished product meets all required quality and performance standards before delivery to the customer. It involves conducting flight tests as per the PPC schedule, maintaining accurate test records, and reporting results to stakeholders. FTQC is responsible for supporting dry runs for new builds or modifications, monitoring CTQ parameters, raising NCRs, and improving testing effectiveness. FTQC also participates in DRS, project kick-off meetings, and ECR discussions, ensures team competency and business continuity, and maintains 5S, safety, and quality standards in testing areas.

Roles and Responsibilities of FTQC

  • Execute Flight test as per the Production Planning and Control (PPC) schedule.
  • Maintain complete and accurate records, and on-time reporting to all stakeholders.
  • Assess skill gaps within team and implement initiatives to close them through upskilling or hiring.
  • Ensure availability of second line for business continuity for all key activities.
  • Ensure dry run and document updates for any new build or new modification along with associated CTQ (Critical to Quality) characteristics.
  • Suggest and implement initiatives to improve the quality and effectiveness of testing.
  • Raise NCRs (Non-Conformance Reports) whenever applicable and on-time closure of NC actions.
  • Actively participate in customer order discussions such as DRS (Delivery Requirement Sheet), Kick-off meetings, and other project reviews.
  • Monitor and ensure adherence to 5S, safety, and housekeeping standards in the inspection and testing areas.
  • Support Engineering such as ECR (Engineering Change Request)
